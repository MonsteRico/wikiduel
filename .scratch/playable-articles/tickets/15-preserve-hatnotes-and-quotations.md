# Preserve Hatnotes and Quotations in Playable Articles

Status: needs-triage
Scope: MVP required
Category: enhancement

## Parent

- [`playable-articles/10`](./10-render-typed-article-documents.md)
- [Playable Articles spec](../spec.md)

## Problem

Playable Article normalization currently excludes Wikipedia `.hatnote`
containers, including “Further information” notices whose eligible article
links could become Navigation Nodes. It also recursively flattens
`<blockquote>` elements and adjacent `.templatequotecite` containers into
ordinary prose, losing the presentation relationship between a quotation and
its source.

The current Playable Articles spec explicitly lists hatnotes among excluded
content, so supporting them requires updating the documented Article Document
boundary as well as the implementation.

## What to build

Preserve supported Wikipedia hatnotes and quotations as explicit, safe Article
Document content. Hatnote links should follow the same canonical resolution and
playability rules as prose links. Quotations should retain enough typed
structure for the client to distinguish and style quoted text, with the final
relationship to adjacent quote citations determined during triage.

Raw Wikipedia HTML must remain an adapter input and must not cross the server
boundary.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Supported `.hatnote` content survives normalization instead of being
      recorded as excluded structure.
- [ ] Eligible article links inside a supported hatnote are resolved into
      Navigation Nodes using the same policy as prose links.
- [ ] Unsupported, malformed, external, and non-playable hatnote links remain
      readable text and do not bypass Navigation policy.
- [ ] `<blockquote>` content retains an explicit typed distinction that lets the
      client style it as quoted content rather than an ordinary paragraph.
- [ ] Quote-source content in adjacent `.templatequotecite` structures is
      preserved according to the relationship decided during triage.
- [ ] The client renders hatnotes, quotations, quote sources, and nested inline
      emphasis or Navigation Nodes accessibly and consistently with the
      Playable Article visual system.
- [ ] Candidate-link extraction and normalization agree on links found inside
      supported hatnotes, quotations, and quote-source content.
- [ ] Focused server and client fixtures cover representative Parsoid hatnote,
      blockquote, and `.templatequotecite` structures.
- [ ] The Playable Articles spec is updated so hatnotes and quotations are part
      of the supported typed Article Document boundary.

## Out of scope

- Preserving Wikipedia’s raw classes, inline styles, or HTML.
- Reproducing every Wikipedia message-box or quotation-template variant.
- Changing which article destinations qualify as Playable Articles.

## Triage findings

- `.hatnote` is currently in the normalizer’s excluded-class policy and is also
  named as excluded content in the Playable Articles spec.
- `<blockquote>` and `.templatequotecite` are not modeled by `ArticleBlock`;
  incidental-container recursion flattens their content into ordinary blocks.
- No matching prior rejection or existing implementation was found.
