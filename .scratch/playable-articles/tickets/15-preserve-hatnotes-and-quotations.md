# Preserve Hatnotes and Quotations in Playable Articles

Status: ready-for-agent
Scope: MVP optional
Category: enhancement

## Parent

- [`playable-articles/10`](./10-render-typed-article-documents.md)
- [Playable Articles spec](../spec.md)

## Problem

Playable Article normalization currently excludes Wikipedia `.hatnote`
containers, including “Further information” notices whose eligible article
links could become Navigation Nodes. It also recursively flattens
`<blockquote>` elements and adjacent `.templatequotecite` containers into
ordinary prose, losing the presentation relationship between a quotation and
its source.

The current Playable Articles spec explicitly lists hatnotes among excluded
content, so supporting them requires updating the documented Article Document
boundary as well as the implementation.

## What to build

Preserve all Wikipedia `.hatnote` variants and quotations as explicit, safe
Article Document content. Hatnote links should follow the same canonical resolution and
playability rules as prose links. Hatnotes should receive one consistent typed
and visual treatment regardless of whether their label is “Further
information,” “Main article,” “See also,” or another upstream variant.
Quotations should retain enough typed
structure for the client to distinguish and style quoted text. Recognize
`.templatequotecite` independently as quote-citation content whose styling
visually associates it with a preceding quotation; do not semantically pair or
validate it against a particular blockquote.

Raw Wikipedia HTML must remain an adapter input and must not cross the server
boundary.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Every `.hatnote` variant survives normalization instead of being
      recorded as excluded structure.
- [ ] All hatnote variants use one consistent typed contract and visual
      treatment without classifying their natural-language label.
- [ ] Eligible article links inside a supported hatnote are resolved into
      Navigation Nodes using the same policy as prose links.
- [ ] Unsupported, malformed, external, and non-playable hatnote links remain
      readable text and do not bypass Navigation policy.
- [ ] `<blockquote>` content retains an explicit typed distinction that lets the
      client style it as quoted content rather than an ordinary paragraph.
- [ ] `.templatequotecite` content is preserved as an explicit quote-citation
      block and styled accordingly, whether or not a preceding blockquote is
      present.
- [ ] Normalization does not need to pair, nest, or validate a quote citation
      against a preceding blockquote.
- [ ] The client renders hatnotes, quotations, quote sources, and nested inline
      emphasis or Navigation Nodes accessibly and consistently with the
      Playable Article visual system.
- [ ] Candidate-link extraction and normalization agree on links found inside
      supported hatnotes, quotations, and quote-source content.
- [ ] Focused server and client fixtures cover representative Parsoid hatnote,
      blockquote, and `.templatequotecite` structures.
- [ ] The Playable Articles spec is updated so hatnotes and quotations are part
      of the supported typed Article Document boundary.

## Out of scope

- Preserving Wikipedia’s raw classes, inline styles, or HTML.
- Reproducing every Wikipedia message-box or quotation-template variant.
- Changing which article destinations qualify as Playable Articles.

## Agent Brief

**Category:** enhancement
**Summary:** Preserve all Wikipedia hatnotes, block quotations, and quote citations as styled typed Article Document content with eligible Navigation Nodes.

**Current behavior:**
The server excludes every `.hatnote` subtree, so useful notices such as
“Further information,” “Main article,” and “See also” disappear and their links
cannot become Navigation Nodes. Blockquotes and `.templatequotecite` containers
are traversed as incidental wrappers, flattening their content into ordinary
prose and losing the visual distinction between quotations, citations, and the
surrounding article. The Playable Articles spec currently names hatnotes among
excluded structures.

**Desired behavior:**
All `.hatnote` variants should survive as one consistently typed and styled
kind of Article Document block. Their eligible article links should use the
same canonical resolution and playability rules as prose links; unsupported or
unplayable links should remain readable text.

Blockquote content should survive as typed quoted content so the client can
style it appropriately. A `.templatequotecite` container should independently
produce a typed quote-citation block. It commonly follows a blockquote and its
styling should make that relationship visually apparent, but normalization
must not pair, nest, or validate it against a preceding quotation. An arbitrary
`.templatequotecite` occurrence receives the same citation treatment.

The server-to-client boundary must remain a typed, allowlisted Article Document;
raw Wikipedia HTML, classes, and styles must not cross it.

**Key interfaces:**

- `ArticleBlock` — represent a hatnote, quotation, and quote citation explicitly
  enough for exhaustive typed client rendering.
- Article normalization and candidate-link discovery — retain the new supported
  structures and agree on which links require canonical resolution.
- Article Document rendering — provide accessible, visually distinct treatments
  while reusing normal Navigation Node behavior.
- Playable Articles spec — replace the obsolete blanket exclusion of hatnotes
  and document the expanded typed block vocabulary.

**Acceptance criteria:**

- [ ] Every `.hatnote` variant survives normalization using one typed contract
      and consistent visual treatment without natural-language label detection.
- [ ] Eligible links inside hatnotes, blockquotes, and quote citations become
      Navigation Nodes under the ordinary canonical playability policy.
- [ ] External, malformed, unsupported, and non-playable links in these blocks
      remain readable text and never bypass Navigation policy.
- [ ] `<blockquote>` content remains explicitly distinguishable from ordinary
      paragraphs and retains supported inline emphasis and Navigation Nodes.
- [ ] `.templatequotecite` content becomes an explicitly styled quote citation
      whether or not it follows a blockquote; no semantic pairing is required.
- [ ] Client output uses accessible structural markup and visually distinguishes
      hatnotes, quotations, and quote citations from ordinary prose.
- [ ] Representative Parsoid fixtures cover multiple hatnote labels,
      blockquotes, adjacent and standalone quote citations, nested supported
      inline content, and unsupported links.
- [ ] The Playable Articles spec reflects these structures as supported typed
      Article Document content rather than excluded subtrees.

**Out of scope:**

- Preserving upstream classes, inline styles, or raw HTML.
- Detecting or assigning special semantics based on hatnote label text.
- Pairing a quote citation with a particular preceding quotation.
- Reproducing every Wikipedia quotation-template layout variant.
- Data-table support, which remains tracked separately in `playable-articles/03`.
